# 02 — CREATIVE PRINCIPLES

## 02.0 — PURPOSE

This chapter defines the philosophical principles
through which an 音のタネ grows into creation.

It defines:

- the source of creation.
- the conditions that allow it to grow.
- the meaning of dialogue,
  resonance,
  silence,
  breath,
  emergence,
  and release.
- the judgment that keeps creation at the center.

This chapter defines meaning.

It does not define how those principles appear
through image,
behavior,
time,
or change.

Their perceptible expression belongs to
03 — CREATIVE PHENOMENA.

Their composition into an experience belongs to
04 — EXPERIENCE COMPOSITION.

## 02.1 — FIRST PRINCIPLE

Creation comes first.

Everything else exists to support it.

### HOW CREATION GROWS

Creation begins with **音のタネ**:
a small possibility formed through lived emotion and experience.

The seed already belongs to the person.

It is not supplied by United Studio or by the Creative System.

Dialogue creates connection around the seed without taking it away from its source.

Through connection,
resonance becomes possible:
between people,
between a person and sound,
and between sound and space.

Resonance gives the seed room to find its own contour.

Growth is not forced expansion.

It is the gradual movement from a possibility that is felt
to a creation that can take form.

Silence is not an absence of creation.

It makes room to notice what has not yet been expressed.

Breath gives this movement time—
between listening and responding,
stillness and movement,
what is held and what is released.

Emergence is the moment when something previously felt
begins to become perceptible.

Release allows the work to leave the environment that protected its growth
and enter another person's experience.

## 02.2 — DISTILLATION

Distillation is not reduction.

It is the pursuit of essence.

It clarifies what must remain
so that an 音のタネ is not buried beneath unnecessary expression.

## 02.3 — BRIDGE TO CREATIVE PHENOMENA

These Creative Principles become perceptible
through 03 — CREATIVE PHENOMENA.

02 defines their philosophical meaning.

03 defines how they may appear
through behavior,
time,
response,
and change.

## 02.4 — EVALUATION

Every screen should leave a feeling.

Not an impression.

Not information.

A feeling.

Every decision must answer one question.

**"Will this make someone want to create?"**

もし答えが No なら、\
そのデザインは、\
どれほど美しくても、\
United Studio のデザインではない。
