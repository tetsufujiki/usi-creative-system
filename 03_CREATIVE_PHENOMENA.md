# 03 — CREATIVE PHENOMENA

## Making the Invisible Perceivable

**Creative Phenomena do not reproduce how the physical world moves.**

**They translate how creation feels into perceptible change.**

## 03.0 — PURPOSE

This chapter defines how the invisible process of creation can become perceptible
through behavior,
time,
response,
and change.

It connects the principles in 02 — CREATIVE PRINCIPLES
to the visual and temporal language that will be developed in later chapters.

It addresses the meaning of an expression,
how it behaves,
how it changes over time,
and when it should remain restrained.

It does not define UI components,
implementation methods,
or technical choices such as CSS,
Canvas,
WebGL,
Three.js,
SVG,
or code.

Those decisions belong to later chapters.

## 03.1 — WHAT CREATIVE PHENOMENA IS

Creative Phenomena begin with a Creative Principle.

Time and change are used only when they carry the meaning of that principle.
A still state can also be a phenomenon when its presence,
pause,
or restraint can be perceived.

Creative Phenomena are not:

- another name for Animation or Motion.
- a catalog of visual effects.
- decoration that keeps a background busy.
- waveforms or particles added to make a site appear musical.
- a faithful simulation of physical phenomena.
- a technical demonstration.
- a display of what AI can generate.

The boundary is meaning.

A behavior belongs here when its source can be traced to creation
and its change can be perceived as part of that meaning.

## 03.2 — PERCEPTUAL ABSTRACTION

Perceptual Abstraction is the primary method of Creative Phenomena.

It does not reproduce the structure or motion of a physical event in full.
It extracts what a person perceives immediately:

- relation.
- response.
- distance.
- tension.
- propagation.
- accumulation.
- change.
- trace.

These qualities are recomposed to express an experience of creation.

Resonance does not require a physically accurate wave.
It is enough for one change to reach another presence,
for that presence to respond after a slight delay,
and for a relation between them to become perceptible.

The creative reality of a phenomenon does not depend on physical accuracy.
It depends on whether its perceptual cause and response feel natural
within the experience of creation.

The result should not become a scientific explanation of its reference.
It may describe a cause that does not exist in the physical world,
provided that a person can understand what changed and why.

## 03.3 — MOBILE AS THE NATIVE SCALE

**It is not a large scene reduced for mobile.**

**It is a perceptual event composed at the scale of the hand.**

Mobile is the native scale of Creative Phenomena.
The screen is narrow,
close to the face,
and touched directly.
Only a small amount is visible at once,
and vertical scrolling gives time a direction.

At this scale,
a small change and its difference from the previous moment can be clear.
A single focus is often more legible than a complex scene.

Creative Phenomena therefore prioritize:

- perceptual change over visual complexity.
- small but legible differences over large physical simulations.
- one clear focus within a screen.
- meaning that remains after color,
  form,
  movement,
  and elements have been reduced.

**Prefer perceptual change over visual complexity.**

**A phenomenon must survive reduction.**

Desktop extends the phenomenon from this native scale.
It does not define the original scene from which mobile receives a smaller copy.

## 03.4 — FROM PRINCIPLE TO PHENOMENON

A working translation frame is:

```text
Creative Principle
↓
Creative State
↓
Perceptual Quality
↓
Behavior
↓
Expression
```

This is not a fixed production sequence.
It is a way to keep expression connected to meaning
without deciding its form too early.

- Resonance is not automatically a ripple.
- Growth is not automatically a plant.
- Seed is not automatically a small circle.
- Breath is not limited to expansion and contraction.

Phenomenon Anatomy may later support this translation.
Current areas for investigation are:

- Trigger.
- Relation.
- Transformation.
- Rhythm.
- Focus.
- Trace.

These are not simulation parameters.
They are perspectives for deciding what a person should perceive.
Their detailed definitions remain open for future development.

## 03.6 — TIME AND BEHAVIOR

Time is not defined only by whether something moves.

It determines when a phenomenon begins,
how it changes,
what it holds,
and how it ends.

The current areas of study are:

- Trigger.
- Delay.
- Duration.
- Pause.
- Accumulation.
- Repetition.
- Settlement.
- Trace.
- Release.

An existing sense of time can be expressed as:

```text
silence
→ statement
→ movement
→ release
```

This sequence is a reference,
not a universal timeline.
Each phenomenon must find the temporal structure required by its meaning.

## 03.7 — PRESENCE AND RESTRAINT

Creative Phenomena do not need to remain active in the background.

A phenomenon can appear,
perform its role,
leave,
and allow stillness to return.
That return is part of the expression.

The current restraint principles are candidates to be tested through real work:

- One Primary Phenomenon per Moment.
- Stillness Is an Active State.
- Motion Must Have a Source.
- Do Not Compete with Information.
- Repetition Reduces Meaning.
- Let the Phenomenon Leave.
- Prefer Perceptual Change over Visual Complexity.
- A Phenomenon Must Survive Reduction.

Their purpose is not to prohibit expression.
It is to preserve attention and meaning.

## 03.8 — PHOTOGRAPHY AND CREATIVE PHENOMENA

**Photography shows what is there.**

**Creative Phenomena reveal what is happening.**

Photography carries the reality of trust:
space,
equipment,
people,
material,
the working environment,
and traces of work that exist.

Creative Phenomena carry the reality of creation:
a feeling beginning to move,
a relation forming,
sound growing,
hesitation gaining contour,
a trace remaining,
or something beginning to emerge.

Neither replaces the other.
They carry different kinds of reality and may support the same experience.

Specific rules for selecting,
composing,
and using photography remain for later chapters.

## 03.9 — AI AS A GENERATIVE PARTNER

AI supports the search for expression.
It is not the subject of Creative Phenomena.

AI may:

- begin with meaning rather than select an existing effect.
- combine different behaviors.
- propose a phenomenon that does not yet have a name.
- explain the meaning of its proposal.
- evaluate whether the proposal has become decoration.
- reduce the expression until it remains meaningful on mobile.

People decide:

- what should be expressed.
- whether the expression feels true to creation.
- what excess should be removed.
- whether it belongs to the brand.
- what it should be called.
- whether it should be used.

The person,
the feeling,
the creation,
the 音のタネ,
and the experience of its growth remain at the center.

## 03.10 — EVALUATION

Each proposed phenomenon should answer:

1. Which Creative Principle is its source?
2. What change is perceived?
3. Why does it require time?
4. Does its meaning survive on a small mobile screen?
5. Does it support rather than compete with information?
6. Does it perform a role that photography or language cannot?
7. Can it be explained only as a familiar visual effect?
8. Does it express a creative experience rather than simulate a physical event?
9. What remains after the phenomenon ends?
10. Would removing it make the experience stronger?

**If removing it makes the experience stronger, remove it.**
